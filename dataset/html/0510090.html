A simple effective method for curvatures estimation on triangular meshes

(Sep, 2005)
Abstract

To definite and compute differential invariants, like curvatures, for triangular meshes (or polyhedral surfaces) is a key problem in CAGD and the computer vision. The Gaussian curvature and the mean curvature are determined by the differential of the Gauss map of the underlying surface. The Gauss map assigns to each point in the surface the unit normal vector of the tangent plane to the surface at this point. We follow the ideas developed in Chen and Wu [2](2004) and Wu, Chen and Chi[11](2005) to describe a new and simple approach to estimate the differential of the Gauss map and curvatures from the viewpoint of the gradient and the centroid weights. This will give us a much better estimation of curvatures than Taubinâ€™s algorithm [10] (1995).
